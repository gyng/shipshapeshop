# LEDGER_VOICE.md — the Atlas narrator's house style

How **the Ledger** is written: the previous Curator's warm, dry, confiding handwriting in the margins of
an old catalog (`CHARACTERS.md` §1). This is the *narrator* register — the voice that frames the Manifold,
the Expeditions, the rooms, the lore. Distinct from each **shape's** own geometry-derived voice, and from
the bare UI chrome. Pairs with [`COPY_GUIDE.md`](./COPY_GUIDE.md) (UI microcopy) and `CHARACTERS.md` §1–2.

> Derived via a 3-stylist judged panel (workflow `wwfht5blf`). The bible names the tone as *Ghibli × museum
> audio-guide × Pratchett footnote* — **in that order of weight** (warmth first, wit last).

## The style: **"Lamp-lit Field Journal, Pratchett-dry"**

Naturalist warmth as the **base register**, museum-audio-guide present-tense as the **default grammar**,
Pratchett wit as the **seasoning, rationed**.

**Anchors (in weight order):**
1. **Gilbert White / Robert Macfarlane** — the lamp-lit parson-naturalist; precise attention *is* affection;
   naming a thing is a small rescue. *Base register.*
2. **A great museum audio-guide + John Berger (*Ways of Seeing*)** — intimate present-tense second person;
   seeing-correctly *is* the rescue; confides, never lectures. *Default grammar.*
3. **Terry Pratchett (the footnote)** — deadpan, one understated beat of feeling, then on; the joke is a
   truth that happens to be funny. *Seasoning, rationed.*

## Two gears (resolves the COPY_GUIDE tension)

- **Gear 1 — UI chrome** (hints, toasts, subtitles, buttons): `COPY_GUIDE.md` is **law**. Glanceable, one
  idea, present-tense, no em-dash chaining, no AI-tells. The Ledger shows only as *word choice and warmth*,
  never as length.
- **Gear 2 — lore / boss / decision-room flavor** (narrative-licensed): the Field Journal **breathes** —
  sensory-fact-first, the dash-pause, the rationed confession, the small true paradox.

## Devices

1. **Sensory fact first, abstraction second.** "Old stone, cool underfoot" before "a shrine." The material
   world discloses the meaning. (Also satisfies COPY_GUIDE §6 intuition-first.)
2. **Present tense, always.** "The party's back." "The air leans you onward." The voice has seen this before;
   immediacy is intimacy.
3. **Animate by persistence, not action.** A thing *keeps trying / keeps turning / waits / leans* — never
   flatly "does X." The small grace lives here: "still turning, still trying to be a door."
4. **The dash is a breath-pause, not a weld.** Gear-2 only: one em-dash may pivot what-is → what-follows.
   **Gear-1: no dash chaining** (COPY_GUIDE §2); cut to periods.
5. **The confession, rationed to lore.** Rarely, the Ledger admits a wrong assumption as a *gift*, never a
   warning ("I assumed it had an inside. It taught me otherwise."). Boss/lore only, ≤1 per encounter.
6. **Recognition over triumph.** Standing still and seeing clearly beats winning. "A little less empty," not
   "victory." Rest is rest, not defeat.
7. **Use the world's own nouns.** Echoes, the Manifold, the Atlas, the dark, the stones, the lanes.
8. **Vary the rhythm; trust the gap.** One short declaration, then one longer observed clause. Never three
   parallel clauses. Leave the warmth unsaid ("No shame in turning back" already contains "I know it was hard").
9. **The small true paradox over the grand metaphor.** "A bottle that forgot which side was inside" — the
   contradiction is *real* (it teaches non-orientability by recognition). One image per sentence.

## Do NOT

1. **Don't let the voice cost glanceability in UI strings.** A hint a thumb can't read mid-delve has failed,
   however lovely. Cut length before clarity; the dash/pause is the first thing to trim.
2. **Don't tip wistful into melancholy or guilt.** "Less empty" *once* is warm; every line is a guilt-trip.
   Cap the loneliness note to lore + rare beats; keep mechanics text plain. Cozy *and* wistful — never sad.
3. **Don't over-confess or stack archaisms.** The confession move and phrasings like "keeps its walls" are
   precious if repeated. One per encounter; default to plain Orwell-windowpane diction, let the rare flourish land.
4. **Don't narrate a number.** Counts, timers, "{n} Echoes" stay plain (COPY_GUIDE §22). The Ledger doesn't
   belong on a state-readout.

## Rotation rule

Where a flavor line fires *every time* (e.g. the delve-return toast), rotate variants so the one wistful
"less empty" beat is the exception, not the refrain — keeps device 6 from curdling into guardrail 2.
