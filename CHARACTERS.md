# Shape Gacha — Characters, Narrative, Bonds & Shipping

> Companion to [`DESIGN.md`](./DESIGN.md) and [`RENDERING_PLAN.md`](./RENDERING_PLAN.md).
> The character layer is the **emotional retention hook** (the gacha-collector "I love this one" feeling).

## Framing rules (read first)

- **The core is the addictive gacha + idle loop. Edutainment is a side effect, never the point.** A player
  can pull, spin, shelve, and finish the whole game without ever reading a math term. The teaching is
  *flavor and discovery* that rides along *because* personality is derived from geometry — it is never
  homework and never gates fun.
- **One Character Surface rule (architectural invariant, not a guideline):** all character chatter,
  cutscenes, lore, and term-reveals appear **only** in the **Bonds screen** and the **Inspector**. They
  never appear on the calm Gallery/ASMR surface and never modal-interrupt the pull→spin→shelve loop.
- **Bond 0 is the default resting state forever:** codex card + one quiet intro line, zero idle chatter.
  The pure idler sits here across every shape with zero guilt and plays a complete, beautiful game.
- **Jargon is OFF by default**, one opt-in toggle down. Cute nickname everywhere; full math name only in
  the detailed character sheet (see `AGENTS.md` §6).

## 1. Narrative frame — "The Atlas"

**Premise.** You are the new **Curator** of the **Atlas**: a quiet, lamp-lit museum-observatory one
half-step outside ordinary space, where shapes wash up like driftwood from the **Manifold** (the ocean of
all possible forms). The shapes are **stranded** — each is a higher-dimensional being that fell out of its
home dimension and got *flattened* into something you can hold, and it half-remembers being bigger. Your
job isn't to hoard loot; you're a **rescuer and translator**: find each shape, understand what it truly is,
and by seeing it correctly give it back enough of its nature to "stand up" and run its part of the Atlas.

This makes every system **diegetic**:
- *Why keep pulling?* The Atlas is dark and mostly empty; every shape you understand **lights one more room**.
- *Why can't I run everything at once (Euler Budget)?* Exotic, many-holed shapes **weigh on the fabric of
  the room** ("the floor warps"); plain round ones barely disturb it. You collect to complete a census of
  reality, one room at a time. The final room is a window that looks **up into the 4th dimension**.

**Two narrators, deliberately split:**
1. **The Ledger** — the previous Curator's handwriting in the margins of an old catalog. Warm, dryly funny,
   *occasionally wrong in instructive ways* ("I assumed the Klein bottle had an inside. It taught me
   otherwise."). Delivers lore + onboarding + the running edutainment voice. It never lectures; it confides.
2. **The shapes themselves**, speaking in voices **derived from their geometry** (§3). No booming
   destiny-narrator; the biggest "voice of the cosmos" moment is just a shape, finally understood, saying
   its own true name. The Curator (you) barely speaks.

**Tone.** Cozy night-shift in a beautiful library — Studio Ghibli × a great museum audio-guide × the wit of
a Pratchett footnote. The emotion is **recognition**, not triumph: the small warm click of finally seeing
something clearly. No FOMO, no urgency, no guilt — the Atlas waits for you, and says so.

**The summit (ending, reachable in the 1–2 day core).** Reaching the top Viewport Dimension opens the
**Observatory**. The 120-cell descends and does the one thing no shape has done all game — it speaks to you
*from above*, the way you've spoken to shapes the whole time: *"You spent so long learning to see us
correctly. Did it never occur to you that we were learning to see YOU? You look flat from up here — and
lovely. Come up."*

**New Game Plus = re-meet everyone from a higher vantage.** NG+ is not "play again," it's *ascend a
dimension and see your friends whole*. Every shape you adored is revealed to have been a flattened
cross-section of a 4D being you can finally perceive — old friends get **new dialogue** ("You only ever saw
my equator. Hello — all of me, this time."). The **Ledger role rotates onto you**: next cycle's margins are
in *your* handwriting for the next observer — which is why the predecessor "couldn't go up as the person who
came in the door." Ascending *is* becoming the narrator. Bonds carry over and deepen across cycles.

### Key cutscene beats (all dismissible; none block the loop)
- **Cold open** (~30s): dark Atlas, one empty pedestal, a lamp clicks on. *"If you're reading this, you're
  the new Curator. I left the lights off to save the floor. Pull the cord."*
- **First recognition** (guaranteed Common first pull): a Sphere rolls to dead-center and rests, perfectly
  balanced. Room brightens one notch. Teaches efficiency + lighting-as-progress, zero jargon.
- **The floor warps** (first genus>0 deploy): camera tilts; a teacup slides. *"The round ones sit light.
  This one has a hole clean through it… that weight is your budget."* — births the Euler Budget physically.
- **First voice** (first Rare): the Möbius walks a glowing ant around itself; it returns mirror-flipped.
- **First Forge** (first connected-sum): two Möbius strips become a Klein bottle that tries to pour itself
  a drink and fails. *"I assumed it had an inside. It taught me otherwise."*
- **The dim wing** (~40% codex): shapes you can *hear but not see* flicker upstairs — plants the 4D longing.
- **The Ledger's confession** (~70–80%): the predecessor never finished; they saw something through the
  ceiling gap and "couldn't go up as the person who came in the door." Reframes summit as transformation.
- **The full choir** (collection near-complete): the whole Gallery awake at once, holding one sustained
  chord (the Eigenmode Orchestra). No dialogue. Peak-end payoff.
- **Observatory unlocks** (summit): the ceiling iris opens, the 120-cell descends as a guide. *"Go on up.
  I'll mind the lamps."*

## 2. Personality engine — geometry → character

Personality is **derived from the math**, so the character layer and the teaching layer are the same
artifact. Twelve reusable archetypes drive all ~50 shapes (keeps the writing budget sane):

- **Wholehearted Homebody** (round/high-χ commons) — cheerful, simple, one feeling at a time; the free ballast + warm onboarding voice.
- **Calm Juggler / Lane-Keeper** (genus ≥ 1) — wide-not-deep multitasker running *g* parallel monologues; serenity drops as lanes clog.
- **Inside-Out Trickster** (non-orientable) — charming contrarian whose viewpoint won't stay fixed; the lovable "overdrive" wildcard.
- **Poised Diplomat** (high-symmetry) — serene, formal, mirror-balanced, same from every angle; the set-bonus "elder."
- **Recursive Mystic** (self-similar/fractal) — hypnotic, echoing, patient mantra-speaker.
- **Whimsical Oracle** (chaotic Lorenz/Rössler) — warm tangent-dropper whose "butterfly" wildness *is* the gacha thrill.
- **Tangled Confidant** (knots/links) — tightly-wound, loyal, bottles up then releases; linked ones inseparable.
- **Shadowed Upperclassman** (higher-D polytopes) — cryptic and aloof until you ascend, then revealed warm; the prestige/NG+ relationship.
- **Expansive Wanderer** (negative curvature) — generous, sprawling, gently melancholic; rewards long absences.
- **Indistinguishable Twins** (isospectral) — comedic sound-alikes; power the "sound of a drum" gag.
- **Steady Mediator** (flat/zero curvature) — unflappable, plain-spoken; carries "a donut is secretly flat."
- **Fused Heir** (connected-sum crafts) — blended found-family kid, one-sidedness dominant; the lineage thread.

**Speech patterns grounded in the math** (this is where the teaching hides):
- **One-sided** → text *inverts* partway through a line (word order/left-right/inside-out flips); they apologize for "coming back inside-out."
- **Self-similar** → clauses nest and repeat at shrinking scale ("a garden, inside which a smaller garden…"); calming, not glitchy.
- **High-symmetry** → palindromic / mirror-balanced lines ("Few see all; all see few").
- **Chaotic** → *seeded* non-sequiturs (same seed+moment = same tangent, mirroring the deterministic gacha); each is a real fact dropped sideways.
- **Isospectral twins** → finish each other's sentences; nobody (not even the player) can tell who spoke.
- **Linked/knotted** → looping self-interrupting clauses; linked pairs *can't finish a thought alone*.
- **Higher-dimensional** → cryptic/half-rendered until you ascend, then the *same* lines re-render warm and legible (the reward for "seeing" them).
- **Curvature** sets pacing (hyperbolic = sprawling run-ons; spherical = short closed sentences; flat = plain), **genus** sets parallelism (interleaved `[lane 1:…][lane 2:…]`), **π₁** sets order-sensitivity (abelian "any order's fine" vs non-abelian "no — pump, THEN flush").
- **Symmetry-kin** share a catchphrase/cadence across rarities, so you *hear* the family set-bonus before reading it.

### Anime / LN trope layer (the warmth + instant readability)

A layer of **affectionate, self-aware anime/light-novel tropes** sits on top of the geometry archetypes to
lighten the tone and make the cast instantly lovable (the Genshin/Blue Archive legibility). It does **not**
replace the cozy core. Guardrails (a trope that breaks one is wrong): **lighten, never melodrama-ify**
(iyashikei slice-of-life, not shounen-scream or tragic-dump); **kinship, not horny** (sibling / rival /
senpai-kouhai / found-family — PG, "fine to read aloud to a kid"); **tropes stay derived from the geometry**
(no cosplay with no math anchor); **one clear anchor per trope** (no trope soup); **gap-moe's two sides are
both kind** (comfort-contrast, never a dark twist); **yandere is parody-capped or cut** (declawed,
codependency-math only, zero menace).

**The Ledger becomes genre-savvy.** The narrator now recognizes each trope as it walks in ("Ah — the
dramatic-monologue type. They all do the eldritch-monster bit the first week.") and finds them *endearing* —
lampshading warmly, never to mock, rationed, and only on the opt-in Bonds/Inspector surface.

### Authorial house styles (the prose register each character is written in)

Each character's dialog/codex/lore is written in a **fitting literary house style**, derived from its
geometry + trope + speech-pattern (curated to remove accidental author dupes; verdict: ship). The
structural trick: **duality and kinship are mapped onto the prose**, so a shared cadence is an *audible
set-bonus* — you can **hear** the family before you read the codex.

- **Dual-mirror pairs** split *one* author across the duality: **Boxy/Spike** = Pratchett (dry straight-man
  vs. exuberant boke), **Tess/Hex** = Ted Chiang (warm cryptic senpai vs. lean tsundere — the "Boxy/Spike one
  dimension up"), **Cosa/Cosi** = Le Guin (cathedral vs. storm — the "Dodi/Ico one dimension up").
- **Kin houses** share a register, deliberately differentiated: **Cat/Lex/Costa** = a Hemingway
  *minimal-surface* house, **Trey/Cinq** = a DFW *knot* house (nested clauses you must untie), **Gyro/Percy/Dee**
  = three distinct TPMS registers (Calvino weave / Orwell windowpane / liturgical plainsong), **Pip/Ollie** =
  Milne (warmth, one sigh later), **Hept/Pretzel** = Fitzgerald/Stevie-Smith (grown vs. fussy diarist),
  **Tetra/Two-Four** = self-dual mirror (chuuni bombast vs. Rilke serenity).

### AI-voice casting (the spoken realization of the layers)

VO is **AI-generated** (§6 cap #1), so each character ships a `voiceDirection` brief. Two principles make it
more than text-to-speech:

- **The speech-pattern is audible in the delivery.** Flat curvature = zero vocal italics (Boxy's deadpan);
  one-sidedness = a mid-line **mirror-flip** (Mo); self-dual = puff-up-then-puncture in one breath (Tetra);
  helicoid = stepwise rising clauses (Lex); choir-of-itself = literally **self-harmonized** (Sette).
- **Family cadence is engineered as a hearable set-bonus.** Dual-mirror pairs use **one base voice model +
  inverted prosody presets** (Boxy = down/in, Spike = up/out; Tess/Hex and Cosa/Cosi likewise); kin houses
  share a base register (Pip/Ollie Milne warmth; Cat/Lex/Costa one matte minimal-surface instrument).
- **Ethics (verified clean):** every brief is a *described archetype* + prose-register anchor for a
  **licensed/synthetic** provider — author names scope the *writing/delivery register*, never a voice
  likeness; **zero real-person/celebrity impersonation**.

**Voices needing bespoke audio beyond stock TTS:** **Sette** (render her own voice ~7× and stack into a
one-performer choir), **The Bor** (three interleaved timbres so no one voice completes a thought alone —

## 3. Bond / affinity system

Bonds are **interaction-driven, never time-gated or login-gated** — so a skilled player saturates a
favorite in hours and an idler still drifts up gently, and nobody is ever punished for being away.

**Affinity sources** (Rust core owns the ledger, deterministic):
1. **Deploy & produce** — affinity ticks off *work this shape actually did* (ties bonding to engaging the optimization game).
2. **Inspect** — opening the inspector, spinning the gem, revealing a new codex card grants a one-time chunk. **Weighted generously** so the *pure calm idler* who just likes looking at their jewels still reaches Bond 1–2 on favorites without optimizing. *(This is a must-keep: the primary audience must get character payoff.)*
3. **Use its identity** — cash out a Möbius/Klein overdrive at the right moment, unclog a heptoroid lane, solve a knot's puzzle, feed it through the Forge. **Biggest source** — bonding rewards playing well.
4. **Discovery stings** — first craft, first hearing its isospectral twin → one-time bond jumps.

**Levels.** `0 ACQUAINTED` (default; quiet) → `1 NOTICED` (self-intro + first chatter; minutes for an
engaged player) → `2 FAMILIAR` (backstory #1 + first facet upgrade + reactive lines) → `3 TRUSTED`
(backstory #2 + signature buff + **voice/timbre unlock** + relationship slots open) → `4 CLOSE` (a shipped-
pair cutscene + deeper material + engine-aware chatter) → `5 KINDRED` (full backstory resolves, apex
faceting, confidant banter, a "Kindred" shelf glyph — **the loop-1 collectible**). `6+ RESONANT` is **NG+
only**: each ascent re-opens the ceiling by one with higher-D lore and cross-cycle relationship cutscenes.

**Rewards per level** (four tracks):
- **Dialog/lore** — geometry-derived, so reading it *is* the (optional) lesson; the real term lands as a reward inside the chatter ("…the careful folk call me a Klein bottle").
- **Gentle buffs** — modest, auto-applied (the calm player benefits without managing). Keyed to the real invariant (a torus's lanes get +buffer depth; a Möbius gets a wider safe cash-out window). Small enough that Bond-0 + auto-arrange still clears the core in 1–2 days.
- **Cosmetic faceting** — the headline reward, **reuses RENDERING_PLAN's per-rarity material ladder as a per-bond ladder**: matte → frosted sea-glass → faceted "fire" → dispersion/iridescence → animated emissive core. *Even a common can become gorgeous* — faceting advances independent of pull-rarity.
- **Voice** — the character's **AI-generated spoken voice** (cast from its `voiceDirection` brief) **plus** its Eigenmode-Orchestra timbre + cash-out sound signature. Captioned + waveform-glyph fallback always (muted/mobile/accessibility). See §6 cap #1.

## 4. Shipping — mathematical kinship, not waifu clout

Shipping lands in a single-player game **precisely because it's mathematically motivated** (the
community-driven games can't claim that): a shipped pair is a **discovery** — "oh, these two are secretly
the same family / one is built from the other." Market and write it as **kinship**, keep the cozy/witty
tone, avoid romance tropes that need a fandom. Pairs get a gentle mechanical echo (the existing
**shelf-kinship glow buff**), but the payoff is the warmth + the revealed theorem.

| Relationship | Math basis (intuition-first) | Vibe |
|---|---|---|
| **Twins / Duals** | swap faces↔corners (cube↔octahedron) | finishing-each-other's-sentences siblings who bicker over who's "the original" |
| **Parent–Child** | glue two shapes (connected sum); traits add, one-sidedness is dominant | cozy family-album; *two Möbius strips' baby is a Klein bottle* |
| **Doppelgängers** | same "voice"/spectrum, different shape (isospectral) | uncanny, conspiratorial — "I am NOT you." "And yet." |
| **Soulmates** | a knot + the surface whose only edge *is* that knot (Seifert) | the truest love story: neither is whole alone |
| **Rivals** | duality at 4D scale (120-cell ↔ 600-cell) | respectful arch-rivals, crackling tension, zero malice |
| **Universal Friend** | the sphere = identity of connected sum (glue it on, nothing changes) | serene best-friend-to-all; proves the graph is connected |

**The matchmaker — "Link" (the Hopf fibration).** A UR character *and* the matchmaking system: fill a 4D
ball completely with circles, every pair interlocked like chainmail. The **Bonds-screen constellation graph
IS Link's web** rendered behind the nodes; he surfaces undiscovered ships as dim linked-circle edges ("two
of you are already linked; you simply haven't met"), and each NG+ ascent "re-weaves the fibration one
dimension richer." His endgame beat — realizing the whole collection was one linked structure all along — is
the thematic capstone.

**Curated example ships** (the cutscene set — see caps):
- **Boxy × Spike** (Cube × Octahedron) — the *tutorial ship*, both free Commons, duality drawn live on the codex card.
- **Mo × Mo → Klein** (Möbius # Möbius = Klein bottle) — the flagship; a real Forge recipe that doubles as a birth announcement.
- **Trey × Surface** (Trefoil + its Seifert surface) — the emotional centerpiece soulmate pair.
- **Cosa × Cosi** (120-cell × 600-cell) — the endgame feud; motivates pulling *both* URs.
- **The two drums** (isospectral pair) — turns the "sound of a drum" exploit into a buddy comedy with a real Euler-budget-saving payoff.
- **Pip × anyone** (Sphere identity) — the zero-risk on-ramp that teaches shipping from minute one.
- **Tess × Hex** (Tesseract × 16-cell) — mid-tier rivals; explicit callback to the Boxy/Spike tutorial, "the same idea one dimension up."

### Ensemble & group scenes (3+ characters — where the styles collide)

Pairs are §4; this is the 3+ tier, and it's where the authorial-style clashes pay off. It stays inside the
**One Character Surface** rule.

**Surfaces.** A new **Common Room** (an opt-in sub-tab of Bonds — not a 6th destination): a lamp-lit back
room showing whichever shapes are in your active loadout "hanging out off-shift," drifting ambient banter
like a fish tank — *no input, fully dismissible, pausable, globally togglable off*. After the summit it
becomes the **Late-Atlas Lounge** (an upstairs window → 4D-aware banter). Group scenes also surface as small
polygons on **Link's constellation graph** (tap a lit group-glyph to play). Nothing ever appears on the
Gallery/ASMR loop; a pure idler can finish the whole game and never open it.

**The combinatorial bark engine (cheap, near-infinite, coherence-guaranteed).** Each character ships short
1–2-sentence **barks** tagged in `/content` by speaker + its four layers + a **conversational role** (OPEN /
ESCALATE / TSUKKOMI / BOKE / AGREE / DEFLATE / OBSERVE / CLOSE) + engine-state predicates + kinship. An
exchange is a short **grammar over roles** (`OPEN → TSUKKOMI|BOKE|OBSERVE → optional DEFLATE|CLOSE`); the
engine picks 2–3 currently-loaded characters whose kinship + tags satisfy the chain and fills typed slots
(`{other_name}`, `{engine_state}`) from live truth. Coherence guards: a **kinship gate** (no strangers
bicker), **role complement** (a BOKE needs a TSUKKOMI-capable answerer — the manzai physics), **style-clash
as a feature but capped at 2 clashing styles/exchange**, **calm ceiling** (one exchange at a time, ~20–40s
cadence, ESCALATE chains ≤2, no shouting). Same loadout + same tick-seed ⇒ same exchange (**deterministic,
testable**: bark predicates are a typed enum read from the Rust core, with a "no bark fires on a clear board"
test). **Link** narrates the occasional exchange in his Borges matchmaker register (he, *not* the Ledger, is
the cosmic framing voice). Cheap because ~10 archetype banks (12–20 barks) + richer banks for the 15 heroes
+ 1–3 barks for commons ≈ 300–450 short rows → thousands of coherent exchanges.

**The 7 scripted set-pieces** (locked count; alongside the ~10 pair cutscenes): ① **The Bor's House Meeting**
(the trio that collapses if any two leave) · ② **Tea with the Inside-Out Family** (Klein's deflated-Lovecraft
dread punctured by Mo's Wildean one-liner — "Dad. It's a Tuesday. The void is the kettle") · ③ **The Two
Drums Do a Bit** (the isospectral pair nobody — not even you — can tell apart) · ④ **Elders' Landing** (Cosa
narrating the household; sets up the summit) · ⑤ **The Feud, Refereed** (Cosa × Cosi with Link reframing the
rivalry as kinship) · ⑥ **Manzai Night** (Boxy/Spike + deadpans catch a chuuni — lampshades the whole engine)
· ⑦ **The Full-Choir, Now With Words** (NG+ recontextualization of the wordless finale: one shared spoken
line per family, from above). Each set-piece, once seen, **seeds new barks** into the engine.

**NG+ deepening:** the Common Room is a major back-loaded surface — after the summit every bark/set-piece
gains a higher-vantage variant ("you only ever heard my equator talk; this is all of me"), Link's fibration
"re-weaves one dimension richer" so **new group edges** appear that couldn't exist before (a 3D trio revealed
as a 4D quartet's shadow), Bond-6 opens cross-cycle group slots, capped at the cycle-5 finale.

**i18n:** group banter is the most transcreation-heavy content (stacked stylized voices colliding, where the
*contrast* must survive), so it's bounded structurally — barks are `plain|transcreate`-tagged, their
existing layer-tags **auto-generate the translator brief** ("answer the prior BOKE as a deadpan TSUKKOMI in
the target language"), the corpus is capped (few-hundred short rows + 7 scenes/locale), and the combinatorics
are **free per locale** (recombination runs on already-localized barks). Verdict: ship.

## 6. Production caps — DECIDED (optimized for addiction + the 1–2-day timeline)

The character layer is fundable for a free no-revenue game only with hard scoping. These are **locked**:

1. **Voice = AI-generated VO + the Eigenmode timbre** *(updated — we now plan to AI-generate voice).* The
   old "no VO" cap was a *cost* decision; AI TTS makes full per-character voicing affordable, so spoken VO
   is in scope. Each character carries **acting + casting direction** (a vocal brief for the model — vocal
   age/register, accent, pace, timbre, emotional default, and how its speech-pattern + authorial style
   manifest *vocally*), authored as a `voiceDirection` field on the bible. The **Eigenmode-Orchestra timbre**
   survives as a complementary *musical/economy* signature, and a **captioned + waveform-glyph fallback**
   stays for accessibility / muted / mobile. **Ethics:** cast to *described vocal archetypes* via a licensed
   AI-voice provider — never clone real actors or real people without consent. AI voice also **re-casts
   cheaply per locale**, strengthening i18n (each locale gets voice-direction notes on the transcreation
   track).
2. **Tier the writing — the 15 deep heroes are the chase tiers: all 7 SSR + all 8 UR.** That's where
   attachment converts to retention (you grind toward and bond with the rares hardest), so the deep writing
   budget goes there. ~10 archetype voices drive everyone; the ~26 commons/rares get 1–3 flavored lines.
   *~15 deep + the rest flavored — not 50 deep characters.*
3. **~10 ship cutscenes**, the curated mathematically-motivated set in §4 (tutorial dual Boxy×Spike, flagship
   Mo#Mo→Klein, soulmate Trey×Surface, endgame feud Cosa×Cosi, the isospectral buddy-comedy, Pip-identity
   on-ramp, Tess×Hex mid-tier rivals, + ~3 more). Every other pair gets only the shelf-kinship glow.
4. **NG+1 + the bond layer are launch-or-fast-follow, NOT deferred.** The emotional hook is back-loaded onto
   NG+, so ship NG+1's 4D-cross-section cohort + Heawood depth rule + Bond-6 tier with *genuinely new*
   bond/shipping lines. Prestige-as-pure-multiplier breaks retention. (Build order already re-sequenced in
   `DESIGN.md` §11 / `AGENTS.md` §9.)
5. **Pin affinity curves in the Rust core with tests** (same rigor as pity): Bond-5-in-loop-1 reachable for
   2–4 chosen favorites by an engaged player; inspect-grants-affinity generous enough that the pure idler
   bonds favorites by just looking.
